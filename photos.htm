BUDDY SPICHER

A native of Dubois, Pennsylvania, Spicher (pronounced “Spiker”) first performed on the WWVA Jamboree in Wheeling, West Virginia, and from there went on to work with some of the great country show bands, including Judy Lynn’s noted western swing show which for years was based in Las Vegas.  In 1958, Buddy made Nashville, Tennessee his home and for over four decades has been one of Nashville’s most important studio musicians and teachers. 

His musical ingenuity has contributed enormously to the commercial success of classic  recordings by artists as diverse as Webb Pierce, Ray Price, Bill Monroe, Kitty Wells, Hank Snow, Patsy Cline, Bob Wills, Linda Ronstadt, Hank Thompson, Henry Mancini, Dan Fogelberg, Joan Baez, George Strait, Loretta Lynn, George Jones, and the Osborne Brothers, to name a few. 

Dubbed early on as “The Jascha Heifetz of the Country Fiddle”, Buddy’s genius for arrangements and harmony has made him a figure of awe among violinists of all styles, and his perfection of tone and intonation established him as the model of America’s most heard, but least known country fiddle player. Versatility is a hallmark of Buddy’s fiddle creativity.  From hoe-downs to jazz; from swing to old time fiddle tunes, Buddy Spicher does it all with great style, an unmistakable finesse, rich harmonies, grace and the ease of a true master of his instrument.

Like many of Nashville’s top recording session musicians, Buddy has always been fascinated by swing and jazz and has played bebop almost since it was invented.  His jazz leanings have helped to loosen up the role of the fiddle in country music.  He has been a mentor to many of the great contemporary fiddlers such as Mark O’Connor, Darol Anger, and Billy Contreras, among many others.

His superb professionalism contrasts sharply with his gentle, self-effacing nature and his extreme modesty;  traits which have won him many devoted friends among his fans.  He tours regularly with Crystal Gayle, arranges, records, and performs with his Nashville Swing Band.  Summers find him at a variety of fiddle camps throughout North America, teaching swing, country, improvisation, and harmony.
